I started my own business in college and spent a decade evolving from freelance web designer to digital marketing manager to executive advisor at a firm founded by futurists. The work was challenging, impactful, and prestigious, but ultimately I missed taking ideas from concept to reality with nothing more than a few sketches, a keyboard, and a natural propensity for lifelong learning.

So I quit my job, went to code school at General Assembly, and now I build responsive sites and apps. Because of my background, I can also create empathy-driven user experiences and manage people, products, and programs. The balance of art and science involved in creating digital experiences allows me to use my natural creativity, work with a variety of teams and skillsets, and engineer solutions at the same time. This career pivot was the best decision I've ever made!

When I'm not debugging code or organizing my Trello board, you can find me hanging with my husband, Mike, and our dog, Juneau, planning a trip to an international destination or National Park, reading books, watching Netflix and HBO, or listening to my latest downloads on PocketCast and Audible.
